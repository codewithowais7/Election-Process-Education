India's Election Process

Step-by-step visual journey from announcement to government formation

1

Election Announcement

~60 days before voting

Election Commission announces the election schedule at a press conference. The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) comes into force immediately on announcement — restricting government expenditure, transfers, and political promises.

📋 MCC Enforced 📅 Schedule Released 🔒 Transfers Frozen
2

Voter List Finalization

~45 days before voting

Final electoral rolls published. Citizens can check their name, correct details, and verify booth assignment at voterportal.eci.gov.in. Booth Level Officers deployed for any corrections.

📜 Electoral Rolls Published 🔍 Voter Search Enabled
3

Nomination Filing

~30 days before voting

Candidates file nomination papers with the Returning Officer (RO). Lok Sabha candidates must deposit ₹25,000 (₹12,500 for SC/ST). Affidavits disclosing criminal records, assets, and liabilities are mandatory.

💰 ₹25,000 Deposit 📄 Affidavit Required
4

Scrutiny & Withdrawal

~25 days before voting

Nominations are scrutinized by the RO. Candidates may withdraw within the withdrawal deadline. Final candidate list published with election symbols — each candidate assigned a unique symbol.

🔎 Nominations Verified 🏳️ Symbols Assigned
5

Campaign Period

~20 days

Candidates campaign through rallies, door-to-door canvassing, print and electronic media. Expenditure limits apply (₹95 lakh for Lok Sabha). All campaign material must show authorization. Cash distribution is illegal.

📢 Rallies Allowed 💸 Expense Limit ₹95L 📺 Media Ads Regulated
6

🤫 Silent Period

48 hours before polling

All election campaigns, rallies, and political advertisements are completely banned in the 48 hours before polling begins. No exit polls may be published. Liquor ban often enforced by state governments.

🚫 Campaigns Banned 📊 No Exit Polls 🍺 Liquor Ban
7

🗳️ Voting Day

THE BIG DAY

Polling booths open 7 AM – 6 PM. Voters cast ballots using the EVM (Electronic Voting Machine) with VVPAT (paper audit trail) for verification. Voter ID card or any of 12 approved photo ID documents accepted.

⏰ 7 AM – 6 PM 🖥️ EVM + VVPAT 🪪 Voter ID Required 🖊️ Indelible Ink Applied
8

Vote Counting

Counting day

EVMs are unsealed at designated counting centres under the observation of candidates' counting agents and ECI officials. Results are declared round by round, constituency-by-constituency. Candidates with the highest votes (FPTP) win.

🔓 EVMs Opened 📊 Round-by-Round 👁️ Observed
9

Results & Government Formation

Post-results

Winning candidates receive Election Certificate from RO. Party or coalition with majority (272+ seats in Lok Sabha) is invited by President to form government. Cabinet appointed, PM takes oath of office.

🏆 Winners Certified 🤝 Coalition Talks 🙏 Oath of Office
